Tourist places in Nashik

Someshwar Temple

Someshwar is on the way to Gangapur dam.There is a temple of Lord Shiva and Lord Hanumana on the banks of the river Godavari.It is mainly famous for waterfall. The area is covered with greenery all over, with a pleasant climate. Tourists can take pleasure of swimming and boating in the river. Read more...

Pandav caves

The group of 24 caves was cut in a long line on the north face of a hill called Trirasmi. There are 24 caves in Pandav leni.Caves carved between the 1st century BCE and the 3nd century CE, though additional sculptures were added up to about the 6th century, reflecting changes in Buddhist devotional practices. Trimbakeshwar temple

Counted as the holiest temple of India, the Trimbakeshwar temple houses one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, a form of Lord Shiva. Built in the 18th century by Maratha ruler, Peshwa Nana Saheb, the temple is a perfect epitome of classic architecture. Trimbakeshwar Temple witnesses lakhs of devotees every year on the occasion of Mahashivratri. Read more...

Anjaneri Hills

Anjaneri Village resides just 4kms before Trimbakeshwar. According to Mythological stories this place is birth place of Lord Hanuman.It has now also become a trekking spot for trekkers. The main tourist attraction is reverse waterfall. the fort is difficult to climb but one can enjoy the hard trekking experience. Read more...

Saptashrungi fort

The Saptashrungi temple is a holy temple of Hindus. This is considered as one of the three Shakti peethas of godess Durga. It is located on Nanduri fort, about 40km away from Nashik. The devotees of Saptashrungi devi celebrate the Navaratri festival every year here. Read more...

Phalake Samarak

Dadasaheb Phalke Smarak spread across a vast extend of 29 acres, and it holds a pretty garden in its courtyard. Placed in the pretty surroundings of a majestic Pandava caves, Dada Saheb Phalke Smarak is one among the busiest attractions in Nasik. Read more...
